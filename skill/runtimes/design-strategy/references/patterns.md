## Trust-Critical Pattern

When users make financial, legal, medical, security, or irreversible decisions, define:

- evidence and source visibility;
- confirmation points;
- reversible actions;
- error language;
- audit or history needs;
- escalation to a human when appropriate.

## Brownfield Pattern

1. Identify existing interaction conventions.
2. Separate stable conventions from known UX debt.
3. Preserve stable behavior by default.
4. Recommend a new direction only where the accepted change requires it.

## Alternatives Pattern

Present alternatives only across meaningful dimensions such as speed versus guidance, density versus simplicity, or automation versus control.

Do not present three cosmetic variants as strategic alternatives.
