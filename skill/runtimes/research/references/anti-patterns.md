# Research Anti-Patterns

## Decorative Competitor List

Failure: naming well-known products without connecting them to the target problem or decision.

Recovery: define comparison criteria from the user problem and remove irrelevant products.

## Unsupported Market Size

Failure: generating TAM, growth, or revenue numbers without a traceable method.

Recovery: omit the number, document the missing evidence, or provide a sourced range with assumptions.

## Current Claims From Memory

Failure: describing present pricing, laws, product capabilities, or leadership from model knowledge.

Recovery: verify through current primary sources or mark the question unresolved.

## Confirmation Bias

Failure: collecting only evidence that supports the user's preferred idea.

Recovery: search for substitutes, switching barriers, failed approaches, and evidence that could reject the assumption.

## Research Without A Decision

Failure: broad browsing that cannot change an artifact.

Recovery: return to the requesting Runtime and define the pending decision first.
