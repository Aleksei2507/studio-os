# External Research Capability

## Purpose

Find and verify current external evidence that may change a product decision.

## Required Access

- Internet or approved external knowledge sources.
- Ability to open primary sources, not only search-result summaries.
- Current date for recency assessment.

## Procedure

1. Define the decision and bounded research questions.
2. Prefer primary, official, regulatory, and direct product sources.
3. Use independent secondary sources when they add market context or challenge a claim.
4. Compare publication date with the date the underlying event or data applies to.
5. Triangulate material claims when practical.
6. Separate sourced facts, inference, assumptions, and unresolved unknowns.

## Evidence

- Direct source links.
- Descriptive source titles.
- Access or verification date.
- Claim-to-source connection near each important finding.
- Explicit uncertainty when evidence is weak or contradictory.

## Unavailable Behavior

If external access is unavailable or prohibited, do not generate a current market or competitor assessment from model memory. Report the limitation and identify what can still be analyzed from project evidence.
