# Product Design Standard

## Goal

Turn product intent into a coherent, usable, and domain-appropriate experience.

## Rules

- Design around the primary user outcome and real usage environment.
- Establish clear information hierarchy and one obvious primary action per context.
- Define loading, empty, error, success, disabled, and permission states when relevant.
- Make system status, consequences, and recovery visible.
- Reuse an existing design system and interaction patterns in Brownfield unless a change is accepted.
- Keep visual direction appropriate to the product domain rather than applying a generic trend.
- Design responsive behavior from content priority and device constraints.
- Use familiar controls for familiar actions and avoid decorative interaction that obscures function.
- Treat accessibility, trust, and destructive actions as design inputs, not final QA polish.
- Validate high-risk flows before investing in broad visual detail.

## Evidence

- primary flow and state model;
- accepted Interface Design foundations and platform variants when selected;
- information hierarchy and interaction rationale;
- responsive and accessibility constraints;
- preserved or intentionally changed Brownfield patterns;
- QA evidence for user-visible outcomes.
